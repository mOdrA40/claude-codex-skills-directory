# Repair, Maintenance, and Operational Discipline

## Rules

- Repair and maintenance are part of system correctness, not optional chores.
- Node replacement, rolling maintenance, and topology changes need rehearsed workflows.
- Operational discipline should keep maintenance boring even at scale.
- Shortcuts around repair usually create future correctness pain.

## Practical Guidance

- Make repair cadence and visibility explicit.
- Track streaming cost and maintenance interference on live traffic.
- Test node loss and replacement workflows with real procedures.
- Align maintenance policy with workload criticality and business windows.

## Principal Review Lens

- Which maintenance workflow is most fragile today?
- Can the team explain current repair health clearly?
- What shortcut is likely to create correctness debt later?
- Which maintenance practice most improves cluster trust?
