# Policy, Governance, and Multi-Team Platforms

## Rules

- Governance should constrain dangerous flexibility without killing platform velocity.
- Policy should focus on high-value risks: security, data boundaries, availability, and cost.
- Platform teams should publish strong defaults and reusable patterns.
- Exceptions need clear ownership and review.

## Governance Guidance

- Distinguish platform-approved components from ad-hoc local abstractions.
- Use policy to catch classes of unsafe change early.
- Track ownership of critical stacks, components, and deployment pipelines.
- Retire snowflake patterns deliberately.

## Principal Review Lens

- Which policy reduces real risk versus just adding ceremony?
- Are teams bypassing standards because the golden path is weak?
- What ownership gap most threatens platform safety?
- Is governance scaling the platform or merely documenting drift?
