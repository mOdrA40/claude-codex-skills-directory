# Audit Mode, Drift, and Exception Management

## Rules

- Audit findings should drive action, not endless ignored backlog.
- Exceptions should be explicit, reviewable, and ideally time-bounded.
- Drift between desired platform posture and real cluster state needs owners.
- Policy systems fail when exceptions become the real default.

## Practical Guidance

- Track exception owners and expiration intent.
- Separate legitimate platform gaps from team-side noncompliance.
- Make audit output usable for prioritization.
- Review stale exceptions and persistent audit noise regularly.

## Principal Review Lens

- Which exception is quietly creating the most risk?
- Are audit results informing change or just producing dashboards?
- What drift is most dangerous to ignore?
- Which exception workflow most needs stronger governance?
