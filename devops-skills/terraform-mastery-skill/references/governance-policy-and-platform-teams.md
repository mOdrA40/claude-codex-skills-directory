# Governance, Policy, and Platform Teams

## Rules

- Governance should create safer defaults without freezing delivery.
- Policy should focus on high-value guardrails: security, data boundaries, cost, and availability.
- Platform teams should publish opinionated standards where leverage is highest.
- Exception handling should be explicit, reviewable, and temporary when possible.

## Platform Patterns

- Curated modules reduce unsafe copy-paste.
- Policy-as-code should prevent dangerous classes of change early.
- Team ownership needs to be visible in repositories, states, and pipelines.
- Golden paths should remain flexible enough for legitimate variation.

## Principal Review Lens

- Which policy creates real risk reduction versus compliance theater?
- Are teams bypassing standards because the golden path is weak?
- What platform contract is unclear enough to generate snowflakes?
- Where is governance too loose or too rigid?
